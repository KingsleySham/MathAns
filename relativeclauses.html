The Architectural Approach

Five Pillars of Mastery

foundation

Defining Clauses

Who, that, whom, whose, which, where, when, why — providing essential information with no commas.

adjust

Non-defining Clauses

Extra (non-essential) information set off by commas. Never use "that" in non-defining clauses.

link

Preposition Structures

Formal preposition + whom/which: to whom, at which, in which. Quantifiers: some of whom, none of which.

compress

Reduced Clauses

Drop the relative pronoun + auxiliary to create a participial phrase: The man sitting by the door…

edit_note

Adjectival Phrases

Relative clauses reduced to adjective phrases: The boy (who is) afraid of dogs… — concise and precise.

star

The Polish

Comma rules, "that" vs "which", contact clauses, and the quantifier patterns — most of which, none of whom — that distinguish top-band writing.
