B-Q1 The Lee Family Trip to Japan 15 marks ▼
📖 Scenario: The Lee Family Trip to Japan
Questions (a)–(d) refer to this scenario
(a) Define "tourist" and "excursionist". Give ONE example each. [4 marks]
1 mark per definition + 1 mark per example
Tourist (1 mark)

A person who travels to destinations outside his/her residence and working place, and stays for at least 24 hours, for the purpose of leisure or business. (§1.3.1)

Example (1 mark)
A Hong Kong resident spending 3 nights in Tokyo on holiday. (Accept any valid example of an overnight stay away from home country/usual environment.)
Excursionist (1 mark)

A person who temporarily visits a destination and stays for less than 24 hours, for the purpose of leisure or business, but not for transit. (§1.3.1)

Example (1 mark)
A Hong Kong resident visiting Shenzhen for shopping and returning home the same day. (Accept any valid same-day trip.)
(b) Identify the type of tourist (Cohen's) with TWO pieces of evidence. [3 marks]
Organised Mass Tourist (1 mark)
Evidence 1 (1 mark)
"The entire trip was booked through a travel agency" — dealt with in a routine way by tourist establishments; itinerary decided in advance.
Evidence 2 (1 mark)
"The itinerary was fully pre-arranged with no changes allowed" — tourists have almost no decisions on their trip; staying in their environmental bubble.
(c) Identify the type of attraction for (i) Osaka Castle and (ii) Traditional Japanese dance. [4 marks]
(i) Osaka Castle — Historic attraction / Historic site (1 mark)

Reason (1 mark): It is a famous historic building with special significance in relation to historical events; included in the handout's "Historic Attractions" category. (§1.8, p.28)

(ii) Traditional Japanese dance — Cultural attraction: Music and dance (1 mark)

Reason (1 mark): "Music and dance are probably the most entertaining and appealing cultural attractions for tourists." (§1.7 / 1.8, p.30)

(d) Identify ONE Tourism Promoter and ONE Tourism Service Supplier with explanations. [4 marks]
Tourism Promoter: Travel agency / Tour guide (1 mark)

Explanation (1 mark): Travel agencies provide services such as making travel arrangements and giving advice on tourism-related matters. They are in the first layer (closest to travellers) of the Integrated Model. (§1.2, p.3)

Tourism Service Supplier: Airline / Hotel (1 mark)

Explanation (1 mark): Airlines and hotels provide transportation/accommodation services to travellers, usually independently. They are in the third layer (Tourism Service Suppliers) of the Integrated Model. (§1.2, p.3)

B-Q2 Top 5 International Tourist Arrivals 2019 15 marks ▼
📊 Scenario: Top 5 International Tourist Arrivals 2019
Questions (a)–(d) refer to this data source
(a) Define "international tourism" and give ONE example from the source. [2 marks]
Definition (1 mark)

International tourism involves trips between 2 countries. International tourists are those who travel to a country other than the one in which they normally live. (§1.4.1, p.9)

Example (1 mark)
A Hong Kong resident visiting France / A Japanese tourist visiting the United States. (Accept any valid cross-border example using countries from the source.)
(b) From China's perspective, Chinese tourists visiting France represent which type of tourism? [2 marks]
Outbound tourism (1 mark)

Explanation (1 mark): Visits by Chinese residents to France (another country) represent China's OUTBOUND tourism. From France's perspective, these visitors are inbound tourists. (§1.4.2, p.10)

(c) Identify THREE pull factors attracting tourists to France. Explain each. [6 marks]
2 marks each: 1 for identification + 1 for explanation. Accept any three.
Pull Factor 1: Natural scenery / Climate

France has beautiful landscapes (Alps, Loire Valley) and a pleasant climate. Tourists seek attractive scenery and comfortable climatic conditions for their holiday. (§1.8 / 1.11)

Pull Factor 2: Historic and cultural attractions

France has world-famous historic sites and art museums (e.g. the Louvre, Palace of Versailles, Eiffel Tower). Tourists are attracted by the beauty of architecture and art, or for their historical/cultural meaning. (§1.8)

Pull Factor 3: Food and cuisine

French cuisine is internationally renowned. "Trying new food is a novel experience that is enjoyed by most tourists." (§1.8, p.30) Food differences are one of the most interesting ways to learn about another culture.

Other acceptable pull factors: favourable exchange rates; strong political/cultural ties; famous events (e.g. Cannes Film Festival); combination of natural and man-made attractions.
(d) Explain whether the Hong Kong resident is classified as a "tourist" using THREE defining factors. [5 marks]
Factor 1 — Length of stay (1 mark)

Stays 10 days, which is less than one year. The visit is temporary and short-term. ✓

Factor 2 — Purpose of visit (1 mark)

Main purpose is sightseeing (leisure), NOT earning income at the destination. ✓

Factor 3 — Distance travelled (1 mark)

Travelling from Hong Kong to France is outside their "usual environment" — not a routine community trip between home and workplace. ✓

Conclusion (2 marks)

The resident meets ALL three defining factors and IS classified as a tourist. (§1.3.2, p.8)

B-Q3 Eco-Tourism in the Amazon 15 marks ▼
🌿 Scenario: Eco-Tourism in the Amazon
Questions (a)–(d) refer to this scenario
(a) Identify the form of tourism. Explain with TWO pieces of evidence. [3 marks]
Eco-tourism (1 mark)
Evidence 1 (1 mark)
"All income from entrance fees and donations is used for conservation of the rainforest." — Eco-tourism income is spent on conservation; it is "a force for conservation and preservation of nature." (§1.7, p.16)
Evidence 2 (1 mark)
"Travellers are reminded to minimise their environmental impact throughout the trip." — Eco-tourists "will minimise their impact on the environment." (§1.7, p.16)
(b) Identify THREE types of tourist attractions. State natural/man-made and give evidence. [6 marks]
2 marks each: 1 for type + natural/man-made, 1 for evidence. Accept any three.
Type 1: Natural attraction — Fauna / Wildlife (natural)
Evidence
"Tourists can observe rare animals such as jaguars, river dolphins, and exotic birds in their natural habitat." (§1.8 Natural vegetation and wildlife, p.22)
Type 2: Natural attraction — Natural vegetation / Tropical rainforest (natural)
Evidence
"Amazon Rainforest in Brazil" — lush tropical vegetation. Tropical rainforests are listed in the handout as tourist attractions. (§1.8, p.25)
Type 3: Cultural attraction — Handicrafts / Traditions (man-made)
Evidence
"Local indigenous communities…demonstrate traditional cooking and craft-making skills." — Handicrafts and lifestyle are cultural attractions. (§1.8, p.29–30)
(c) According to Plog's classification, which tourist type does this tour target? Give TWO characteristics. [3 marks]
Allocentric tourist (1 mark)

Characteristic 1 (1 mark): "Prefer non-tourist destinations and few developed tourist attractions." / "Enjoy discovering new destinations before others have visited them." The Amazon is an off-the-beaten-path destination. (§1.10, p.41–42)

Characteristic 2 (1 mark): "Prefer simple services, such as adequate to good accommodations." Evidence: "Stay in basic but adequate accommodation rather than luxury hotels." (§1.10, p.42)

(d) State ONE push factor and ONE pull factor for this eco-tour. Explain each. [3 marks]
Push factor (0.5 + 0.5 mark)

Any valid internal motivation, e.g. desire for adventure / desire to escape urban pressures / desire for social interaction / desire for self-education. Push factors are "intangible desires of the individual traveller." (§1.11, p.45)

Pull factor (1 + 1 mark)

Any valid destination attribute, e.g. unique Amazon wildlife / lush tropical rainforest / cultural experience with indigenous communities / conservation opportunities. Pull factors are the "attractiveness or drawing power of the destination." (§1.11, p.45–46)

⚠️ Do NOT award marks if student confuses push and pull factors (e.g. gives an internal motivation as a pull factor or vice versa).

B-Q2 Papua New Guinea Resort — Service Gaps 15 marks ▼
🏨 Scenario: Mr. & Mrs. Cheung's stay at a Papua New Guinea resort
Questions focus on service quality gaps and tourism types
(c) Identify THREE service quality gaps with explanation and evidence. [9 marks]
3 marks each: 1 for gap name + 0.5 explanation + 0.5 evidence × 3
Gap 1: The Communication Gap (1 mark)

Explanation: The gap between what the company promises in its marketing communications and what is actually delivered.

Evidence
"The resort's website promised a complimentary welcome drink upon check-in, but no drink was offered when they entered the room." — the communicated service did not match the delivered service.
Gap 2: The Service Performance Gap / Service Delivery Gap (1 mark)

Explanation: The gap between the service standards set by management and the actual service delivered by frontline staff.

Evidence
"Due to a lack of staff, they waited over 45 minutes before being assigned a room." — actual service delivery fell below the expected standard for a five-star resort.
Gap 3: The Knowledge Gap / Customer Expectations Gap (1 mark)

Explanation: The gap between customer expectations and management's perception of those expectations.

Evidence
"Staff brewed famous local hot coffee…but they were not keen on it due to the climate." — the manager misjudged what guests would enjoy in a hot tropical climate, suggesting a gap in understanding customer expectations.
(d) Identify capital and currency of Papua New Guinea. Explain inbound or outbound from HK's perspective. [4 marks]
Capital: Port Moresby (1 mark)
Currency: Papua New Guinean Kina (PGK) (1 mark)
Type: Outbound tourism from Hong Kong's perspective (1 mark)

Explanation (1 mark): "Visits by residents of [a] country to another country is [that country's] outbound tourism." Mr. and Mrs. Cheung are Hong Kong residents travelling to Papua New Guinea. This represents Hong Kong's outbound tourism. From Papua New Guinea's perspective, they are inbound tourists. (§1.4.2, p.10)
