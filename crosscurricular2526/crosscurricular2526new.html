Qualitative observations actual results
Etiolation in all groups (low indoor light) · Cotton yellowing in Sol A · Seeds blackened and failed in Sol B
Control (0 g)Germination by day 3–4. Long pale shoots (etiolated indoors). Roots healthy white. Cotton remained white throughout.
Solution A (4.144 g)Germination delayed ~1–2 days. Shoots shorter, yellowing visible. Cotton turned yellow — Pb²⁺ staining. Growth ~50% of control.
Solution B (12.424 g)Most seeds failed to germinate. Remaining seeds blackened and shrunken. Only a few short shoots emerged. Severe toxicity.
Confounding variable — etiolation: All groups including the control produced pale yellow elongated shoots due to limited indoor light. This is not caused by lead nitrate. Next time, place dishes near a window or under a grow lamp.

Conclusion
9
Negative correlation confirmed
As Pb(NO₃)₂ concentration increases, mung bean shoot height decreases. Lead ions disrupt root cell division and chlorophyll synthesis, inhibiting germination and growth. Results support the hypothesis that lead nitrate is toxic to Vigna radiata even at moderate concentrations.
